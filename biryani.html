Biryani

About Biryani

Biryani is a mixed rice dish originating from the Indian subcontinent. It is made with aromatic basmati rice, spices, and usually includes meat (such as chicken, lamb, or beef) or vegetables. The dish is known for its rich, flavorful taste and is often served with raita or a boiled egg.

Ingredients

  • Basmati rice
  • Meat (chicken, lamb, or beef)
  • vegetables
  • Yogurt
  • Spices (cumin, coriander, cloves, etc.)
  • Onions
  • Garlic and ginger

Process

The preparation of biryani involves marinating the meat with yogurt and spices, then layering it with partially cooked rice. The dish is then slow-cooked to allow the flavors to meld together. It is often garnished with fried onions and fresh herbs.
